Project Introduction

The project uses point cloud virtualization to provide a new travel experience. It mainly shows the buildings of the School of Environmental and Forest Sciences. The three parts of the campus of the University of Washington Forestry Department are Anderson Hall, Winkenwerder Hall, and Bloedel Hall. The view can be adjusted to various angles to achieve the most immersive experience.

Point Cloud Map

Anderson Hall

  • Dates: constructed 1924-1925
  • Designers: Bebb and Gould, Architects; Charles Herbert Bebb; Carl Freylinghausen Gould Sr.
  • Donor: Mrs A.H. Anderson
  • Interior Features: an auditorium, exhibition and clubroom and a reading room

Bloedel Hall

  • Dates: constructed 1970-1971
  • Designers: Robert Allan Chervenak; Ross W. Copeland Jr.; Austin Grant; Richard Haag
  • Richard Haag and Associates, Landscape Architects, designed the courtyard formed among Bloedel, Winkenwerder and Anderson Halls.

Winkenwerder Forest Sciences Laboratory

  • Dates: constructed 1962-1963
  • Designers: Robert Allan Chervenak; Ross W. Copeland Jr.; Harvey Dodd; Austin Grant
  • Award: Merit from the American Institute of Architects (AIA) in 1966
